Initially, I found it quite hard to get into this show. The rapid editing is definitely something you will have
to get used to, same as the general pacing. When I started this show about a year ago, I was pretty overwhelmed
by this. I usually like slower paced movies and shows that give the viewer time to breathe. This show is pretty
much the opposite of that. Now, after rewatching it, I have to admit to myself that I was wrong. Such a fast pace
can work. It does not feel gimmicky, or as if you are just trying to stimulate the viewer. Why? I think it
is because it seems "authentic" to the environment the show takes place in for the most part. Since the entire
first episode (as far as I can recall) takes place in the kitchen, the fast pace makes sense. But not all of this
show involves the kitchen. There are slower, character driven moments that take place outside. There, the pacing
is drastically reduced. Sadly, we only come across these moments from the second episode onwards, giving the
false impression that the whole show will be none stop fast paced, at least to the people who stop after the
first episode, like I did.

That aside though, I think this show looks absolutely incredible. Maybe it is because I am from a small town
and have barely been to any big cities, but I love how they portray Chicago in this show, with the trains, the
industrial sites, alleyways etc.
There are also a lot of beautiful shots with natural lighting, like at the start (and end) of episode 3, with
Carmy at the beach gazing at the Sunrise.

Character development is also done to perfection. Let us examine the character of Tina, as an example for this:
She starts off being very much against Carmy's attemps to reinnovate the restaurant, even going as far as to
sabotage his workspace. She also does not respect Syndey at all. All of this, added onto the fact that she is
pretty much working against the desires of not only Carmy, but also the viewer, has made me hate her like no
other character during the first few episodes. At least Richie was entertaining while being a douche, but Tina
was just an unpleasant individual.
Then, at the end of episode 4, almost all of that hatred is resolved within a single scene, making Tina respect
Sydney for her efforts and talent.
The fact that the show is able to do such a total switchup shows how well written it is.

I know that onetakes are a bit gimmicky, but it fits the episode in my opinion.
