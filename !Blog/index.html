Starting this blog, new state of living

This the first blog post I have ever made. Still, I want to spare you my hesitative thoughts regarding this, since - as you
can see - I did end up doing this.

When I described to ChatGPT what I was doing and asked it for a name, it gave me "limbo letters". Whether I end up sticking with it
or not, I think that it really fits my current state of living. Never in my life have I been so uncertain of what even the next month might
look like. All this thinking about the future has actually led to me feeling weirdly unpresent during a lot of the long awaited stuff that
I've looked forward to, like my graduation celebration or the trip to Spain with my friends, the first time in my life I've ever left my
home country. Now that both of these things are over, I end up looking back realizing how fast the time went, almost melancholically.
Even weirder is that during these things, instead of enjoying them for myself - right now - it felt like I was doing all of it to gather
memories and stories for my future self.

I think that we've been told so many times that "you never know the good times until they are over", we actually end up compromising
our present experiences by trying to feel a sense of gratitude that "these are the good times now and therefore I owe it to my future
self to enjoy them as much as I can, for I know that worse times will come". I think this leads to a sort of "performative joyfulness".

Trying to feel present in these moments makes them almost feel like a task that I have to check off. Maybe it's because I focus too much
on being present, because I do value that very much. Maybe I care too much about what future me will think of current me for not being
present. And right now, I am mad at past me for being mad at not being present and therefore being even less present. I feel mad to
have served only for the future and that I, as a tradeoff, missed out on the deeper experience, may that one have been less focused,
but much more authentic.

I think a better version of that first quote would be:

"Bad times will come, but they are not here now, so don't feel obligated to enjoy the good times.
You'll understand the value of them during the bad ones."
